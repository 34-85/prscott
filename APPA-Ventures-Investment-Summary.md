# APPA Ventures — Investment Summary

*Board investment memo · one-page · illustrative planning figures (see full Operating Plan §A–§C for detail) · June 2026*

---

**The ask:** Authorize the formation of **APPA Ventures, Inc.** (a wholly-owned, taxable C-corporation) and commit **~$3.0M of reserve capital, staged over three years**, to launch two businesses: an AI-enabled pet-market **data platform** and a hyper-local pet-owner **media network**. Fully funded from APPA's ~$16.7M net assets; **no external capital required.** *(Capital and P&L reflect the line-itemed bottoms-up budget in Operating Plan §D.)*

**The thesis:** Diversify APPA beyond its two-product concentration (trade show + periodic research), **convert advocacy from an expense into a profitable channel**, deepen dues retention, and build a proprietary pet-data moat — all inside a structure that protects APPA's tax exemption and remits profit upstream as tax-free dividends.

### The two businesses

| Business | What it is | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Pet Intelligence** (§A) | AI-enabled pet-market data subscription (B2B), built on APPA's owned NPOS + licensed POS | Near-term profit engine (~65% gross margin); dues-retention lever |
| **Pets of [City]** (§B) | Hyper-local pet-owner media network (B2C); AI test markets → staffed editors | Audience, first-party data, advocacy-as-profit (housing, independent retail, shelters, vets, member brands) |

### Use of funds (~$3.0M staged)

Entity formation & governance · Ventures President + lean shared platform (data/AI, ad sales, content-ops, customer success) · personnel (35 FTE by Year 3, incl. 16 commission-weighted community editors; 30% benefits load) · Pet Intelligence partner-launch & data licensing · Pets of [City] AI test markets + graduating winners to staffed editors · subscriber acquisition.

### Returns to APPA

- **Cash:** ~$0.5M returned cumulatively by end of Year 3 (brand + data royalty), with **dividends beginning in Year 4** and accelerating in **Years 4–5** as Pets of [City] markets mature past ~36 months and Pet Intelligence compounds; a profitable, growing subsidiary with real enterprise value.
- **Strategic (the larger return):** higher **dues retention** (platform-active members renew at higher rates), a **proprietary data moat** vs. commercial vendors, APPA's **first-ever B2C consumer reach** (a channel for Pets Add Life), and **advocacy converted to profit** — with the local economic data that makes APPA's housing and anti-retail-ban positions more persuasive.

### Structure & governance

Wholly-owned **C-corp**; each business in its own subsidiary LLC (clean partner/JV at the unit level). APPA controls via board majority + reserved matters (brand, data rights, advocacy alignment, capital); written guardrails (**no veterinary medical advice**; nothing contrary to APPA policy). Commercial comp/equity attracts operators a (c)(6) cannot. After-tax profit flows up as **tax-free dividends**.

### Key risks & mitigations

Exemption protection → the C-corp *is* the mitigation. · Data-rights for AI → negotiate up front; launch on owned NPOS. · Local-media sales doesn't scale without people → self-serve directory + central national sales. · Capital exposure → stage-gated funding, cheap AI options, optional partner co-investment. · Downside case (conversion ~30% lower, slower PoC ramp): cash-positive slips to Year 5, peak capital ~$3.6M.

### Recommendation

**Approve formation and the ~$3.0M staged commitment** (drawn over three years, gated to milestones). The downside is a capped, reserve-funded investment APPA can comfortably absorb (~$2.3M net at risk against $16.7M net assets); the upside is a diversified, higher-margin, mission-advancing business that strengthens the core association — funded entirely from an under-utilized balance sheet.
