Openings, closings, and the things changing before anyone else says so.

Hidden Gems

The shop at the halfway point

You've passed it a hundred times heading up Lookout. Mountain Memories has sat on Scenic Highway since 1928 — back when it was the “Halfway Shop,” where horses watered halfway up the mountain. Behind the counter is Jeanne Crawford, granddaughter of Leo Lambert, the man who discovered Ruby Falls that very same year.

Tour buses blow right past on their way to the big attractions. Don't. Stop in, and ask her about the carriage days — she tells the stories nobody else stops to hear. That's the whole point of this section.

The Open Door  ·  new

Because “is it good?” isn't the only question

A section we'll run all year. Can you get in with a wheelchair? Will it work for a kid who's overstimulated by noise? Is there anything worth ordering if you don't drink? We'll answer those, one place at a time — and we need your help.

This week, an easy win: Coolidge Park & the Tennessee Riverwalk on the North Shore — flat, paved, stroller- and wheelchair-friendly, and free, with the antique carousel and the splash fountain. A genuinely come-as-you-are morning for every body.

Where does Chattanooga get access right? Reply and nominate a spot — you'll shape this section.

The Standing Invitation

Do this one thing this week

Not a calendar. One assignment, and the why.

Sunday, go to the Chattanooga Market at First Horizon Pavilion (11–4). Skip breakfast. Walk the whole thing once before you buy a single thing — then go back for the one you couldn't stop thinking about. It's the beating heart of “made here,” now in its 26th season, and half the makers we'll feature this year start at a booth just like these.
