◆ Lead Signal · Triangulation

Manufacturing's great rotation: the EV battery belt is laying off while defense factories can't hire fast enough

No single press release says this — but put the WARN notices next to the hiring waves and the picture is unmistakable. Skilled US manufacturing capital and labor are rotating out of EV/batteries and into defense production.

FADING SK Battery America — ~958 jobs cut in Commerce, GA after Ford canceled the all-electric F-150 Lightning + the $7,500 EV credit lapsed. MED-HIGH
GM Factory ZERO — 1,300 idled at GM's flagship EV-only plant in Detroit-Hamtramck (a $2.2B "electric future" conversion). HIGH
BlueOval SK (KY) — battery-plant layoffs delayed/looming across the "battery belt." MED
BOOMING Anduril "Arsenal-1" — 4,000+ jobs at a 5M-sq-ft drone/missile megafactory near Columbus, OH; opened ~3 months early, the largest job-creation project in Ohio history. HIGH
Saronic — $300M autonomous-warship shipyard in Franklin, LA, ramping after a $1.75B Series D. MED

THE READ  The 2021–22 battery-belt gold rush is unwinding in WARN filings state by state, while defense-tech is hiring factory floor — production engineers, techs, ops managers — by the thousand. The bet underneath the job reqs: the next war is won by whoever can mass-produce drones and hulls. Watch where the laid-off battery technicians land.
