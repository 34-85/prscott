🏡 Neighborhood Spotlight

St. Elmo — life at the foot of the mountain

If you want to know whether you'd love St. Elmo, stand on a porch on a summer evening and look up: Lookout Mountain fills the whole sky. This is one of Chattanooga's oldest streetcar suburbs, and it still moves at a streetcar pace — neighbors on porches, the Incline Railway clanking up the hillside, a Saturday line out the door at Mean Mug. The housing stock is the draw: turn-of-the-century cottages and bungalows, the occasional restored Victorian, on flat, walkable, tree-lined streets that run right up to the trailheads.

Who it suits
Outdoor people, walkable-life seekers, character-home lovers who don't need new construction.
The trade-offs
Older homes mean older systems; tight inventory; you pay for the walkability and the view.

🧭 New in Town

Just landed in Chattanooga? Two things to do in week one: (1) set up EPB — the city-owned utility runs one of the fastest residential fiber networks in the country, so gigabit internet is genuinely a perk of moving here, not a luxury; (2) sort your Hamilton County vehicle registration and check your address's school zone before you fall in love with a house — zones don't always follow the neighborhood line you'd expect.
