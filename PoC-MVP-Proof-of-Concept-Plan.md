# Pets of [City] — MVP Proof-of-Concept Plan (pre-incorporation)

> A lean, ~5-month test to validate the riskiest assumptions **before** incorporating APPA Ventures or committing the staged ~$3M (§D). Run as an **internal APPA project/program — no new entity yet.** Its only job: generate the evidence for a confident go/no-go.

## 1. Purpose & the riskiest assumptions to test

Don't build the company to find out if the model works — test the four things that would kill it:

| # | Hypothesis | Why it's the risk |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | **Demand** — we can acquire engaged local pet-owner subscribers cheaply | If CAC is high, the whole model breaks |
| H2 | **Engagement** — people actually open/read it weekly | Engagement is the leading indicator of both retention and ad value |
| H3 | **Monetization** — local SMBs and ≥1 member brand will pay to reach this audience | Revenue is editor-sold + national; unproven for pet niche |
| H4 | **Operations & quality** — AI-assisted + human-reviewed weekly content is good, factual, and cheap (no YMYL issues), and one editor can run a market | Tests the unit model and the §B guardrails |

## 2. Scope — 3 MVP markets (each tests a different rationale)

Keep it to **three markets** to learn fast without overspending:

| Market | Why this one for the MVP |
|---|---|
| **Austin** | Sample edition already built → fastest to publish; tests pure AI-assisted market |
| **Orlando** | **Local talent identified** → tests the staffed-editor + local-ad-sales motion (the real revenue test) |
| **Denver** | **Confirmed pet-deposit program** → tests the mission-as-content / housing-advocacy angle in an affluent, engaged market |

*(San Antonio + the rest of Wave 1 follow post-incorporation. Three markets is enough to validate all four hypotheses.)*

## 3. Timeline (~5 months)

| Phase | Weeks | Activities | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 · Setup** | 1–3 | Pick placeholder brand; stand up newsletter platform (e.g., beehiiv/Substack-class) + analytics; build content templates & AI-assisted workflow with human review; 3-market content calendars; advertiser target lists; metrics dashboard | Ready to publish |
| **1 · Soft launch** | 4–7 | Begin **weekly** sends in all 3 markets; start paid subscriber acquisition; seed via shelters/partners/social | First subscribers + baseline engagement |
| **2 · Grow & monetize** | 8–16 | Scale acquisition; **Orlando editor begins local sponsorship outreach**; secure pilot sponsors/LOIs; pitch ≥1 member-brand national sponsor; run 1 small community/partner activation | Subscriber growth + advertiser validation |
| **3 · Measure & decide** | 17–22 | Analyze vs. go/no-go thresholds; write board decision memo | **Go/no-go** on incorporating Ventures + committing ~$3M |

## 4. Success metrics & go/no-go thresholds

Measured per market by end of pilot (~month 5). Benchmarks anchored to 6AM City economics (CAC <$1, LTV >$25) and the §B graduation logic (~8–12K subs to staff).

| Metric | Target (per market) | Read |
|---|---|---|
| **Subscribers** | **3,000–5,000** | On a trajectory toward the ~8–12K staffing threshold |
| **Subscriber CAC** | **≤ $1.50** | Validates the acquisition model (H1) |
| **Open rate** | **≥ 40%** | Strong for local newsletters (H2) |
| **Click rate** | **≥ 3%** | Real engagement (H2) |
| **Unsubscribe / send** | **< 0.5%** | Content-market fit |
| **Advertiser validation** | **≥ 2–3 paid pilot sponsors or signed LOIs/market; ≥1 member-brand national sponsor interested** | Monetization proof (H3) |
| **Content quality** | **Zero factual/YMYL incidents** | Guardrails hold (H4) |
| **Editor capacity (Orlando)** | One editor sustains weekly content **+** sells local sponsors | Unit model works (H4) |

**Decision rule:** **GO** to incorporate + fund if **≥2 of 3 markets** clear the subscriber + engagement thresholds **and** advertiser validation is met in **≥1 market** (ideally Orlando). Otherwise iterate or stop — cheaply.

## 5. Budget (~$125–150K, 3 markets, 5 months)

| Line item | Allocation |
|---|---|
| Paid subscriber acquisition (3 markets) | $45,000 |
| Local editor (Orlando, part-time) + content contributors (Austin/Denver) | $35,000 |
| Newsletter platform, AI content tooling, analytics/CRM | $10,000 |
| Brand placeholder, landing page & email templates (design) | $10,000 |
| Project lead (fractional PM / existing APPA staff time) | $20,000 |
| One community/partnership activation | $7,000 |
| Legal/advisory (sponsorship terms; no entity) | $5,000 |
| Contingency (~10%) | $13,000 |
| **Total** | **~$145,000** |

**Leaner alternative (~$85K):** 2 markets (Orlando + Austin), heavier AI / lighter paid acquisition — slower learning but lowest cash.
**Funding:** an operating-budget line item / board-approved pilot, **well under any threshold requiring entity formation** — and a rounding error against the ~$3M it de-risks.

## 6. Team (lean, mostly contract)

- **Project lead** — existing APPA staffer or fractional PM; owns the pilot and the decision memo.
- **Orlando community editor (PT)** — the identified local talent; runs Orlando and helps codify the playbook.
- **Content/AI contractor** — AI-assisted drafting + **human/editorial fact-check** across all 3 markets.
- **Freelance designer** — brand placeholder, templates, landing page.
- **Performance-marketing contractor/agency** — paid acquisition.
- **Advisor (optional)** — a local-newsletter/media operator for guidance.

## 7. Structure during the MVP (important)

- **No new entity.** Run inside APPA as a program/pilot to avoid premature formation, tax, and overhead.
- **Keep commercial activity contained:** treat early sponsor dollars as **program sponsorship** and keep volume modest, so unrelated-business exposure stays minimal during the test. Scaled ad sales and the data integrations wait for **APPA Ventures** post-go.
- **Hold the guardrails from day one:** no veterinary/medical advice; factual, sourced editorial; sponsors clearly labeled.

## 8. What the MVP de-risks → the decision it feeds

A successful pilot converts the §D investment case from projection to evidence: real CAC, real open/click rates, real advertiser willingness-to-pay, and a working editor unit. That is exactly what the board needs to confidently **(a) incorporate APPA Ventures, (b) commit the staged ~$3M, and (c) scale to the full Wave-1 seven markets.** A weak pilot saves APPA ~$3M and a year — the entire point of doing it first.
