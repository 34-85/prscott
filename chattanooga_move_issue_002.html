🧭 Buying Here From Away — a first-timer's field guide

Five things that surprise out-of-state buyers

  1. The mountains change everything about a commute. "15 minutes away" up Signal or Lookout is not 15 minutes in January. Drive it at rush hour before you buy.
  2. Tennessee has no state income tax — a real factor in your true cost of living vs. where you're leaving. Budget the difference into your price range.
  3. Flood zones and old fill. Some pockets near the river and creeks carry flood insurance you didn't plan for. Ask early; it moves the math.
  4. School zones don't follow neighborhood names. Verify the exact zoned schools for the actual address, not the area.
  5. Older homes, older systems. Knob-and-tube, original HVAC, and foundation quirks are common in the charming historic stock — which is why issue 002's sponsor tip is about inspections.

🏡 Neighborhood Spotlight

NorthShore — walk to dinner, paddle before work

Across the river from downtown, NorthShore is the closest Chattanooga gets to "park your car and forget it." Coolidge Park, the river, the Walnut Street Bridge (we're all counting the days until it reopens), and a walkable strip of restaurants and shops are the whole pitch. Homes run from updated cottages on the flats to bigger places climbing toward Stringer's Ridge. It's the neighborhood people move to when they want energy and walkability without downtown's high-rise feel.

Who it suits
Walkers, paddlers, dine-out couples, anyone who wants downtown energy a bridge away.
The trade-offs
Premium per square foot; parking and weekend crowds; the most competitive bidding in town.
