Meet a Maker

Pelfrey Drum Works

Hixson · Jerry “Punkin” Pelfrey

That snare cracking through a set at a Southside bar might have been born in a Hixson workshop. Jerry “Punkin” Pelfrey has been building drums since the '90s; today he makes stave snare drums one at a time — gluing, turning, and tuning each shell by hand — and ships them to players around the world.

It's the opposite of a factory: slow, single-batch, built to outlast the drummer. This is what we mean by “who made this?”

The Building's Biography

1627 Rossville Avenue

Before the new sign goes up, the story of the room.

From 1918 to 2025, this address fed Chattanooga as Zarzour's — four generations of a Lebanese-American family, plate-lunch lines out the door, and a counter where the whole city seemed to take a turn. When it closed last year after 107 years, it was the oldest restaurant in town.

This summer it becomes Pauly's. Same walls, new chapter — and the new owners say they're keeping the bones. We'll be there the morning the screen door swings open again. Every storefront has layers. We'll keep digging them up.

The Standing Invitation

Do this one thing this week

Beat the July heat: a weekday-morning climb up Stringer's Ridge before 9am. It's an in-town trail most visitors never find — twenty minutes of easy switchbacks to a skyline view of the whole city and the river bend. Bring water, leave the earbuds out, and let the city wake up below you.
