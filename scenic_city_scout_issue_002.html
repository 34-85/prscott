Meet a Maker

Niedlov’s Bakery & Café

Main Street · Erik & Lauren Zilen

You crowned its sourdough last week, so let’s talk about the people behind it. Erik and Lauren Zilen took over Niedlov’s in 2016 and turned a beloved bakery into a quiet model of how to run one. They hire people coming out of incarceration, giving folks a real second start; they source from more than a dozen local producers; and they compost something like 1,300 pounds of food waste a month.

It’s the kind of business that makes a city better just by existing — and yes, the loaves are exceptional. Go early; the good stuff goes fast.

Block by Block

This week: St. Elmo

A short tour of one neighborhood at a time.

Tucked at the foot of Lookout Mountain where the Incline Railway begins its climb, St. Elmo still feels like a small town that wandered into the city. The South Broad strip is all locally owned storefronts and a classic-diner pace — the kind of block where the shopkeepers know your dog’s name before they know yours.

It’s having a quiet moment as new independents move in alongside the old guard. Park once, walk the strip, ride the Incline for the view, and you’ve got a full Saturday without ever touching the interstate. Got a St. Elmo favorite we should know? Reply and tell us.

Open Tab

A few small things worth knowing.

A shop to know: I Go Tokyo on Frazier — a tiny, joyful trove of Japanese stationery, cookware, and goods. Easy to lose half an hour in.

A pantry upgrade: Alchemy Spice Co. makes micro-batch seasoning blends right here — the fastest way to put a little Chattanooga in your kitchen.
