Spoiler: a real person who lives here.

Hi — I'm [Your Name], and I write the Scenic City Insider.

Let's get one thing out of the way: the Insider isn't a private-equity rollup or a national chain wearing a “local” coat of paint. It's one person who lives here, in Chattanooga, and loves it. No investors waiting on an exit, no out-of-town newsroom, no algorithm deciding what you see.

A little about me: I spent my career building and running companies, and retired as a CEO. Along the way I wrote four technical books — which is a polite way of saying I'm comfortable going deep on a subject and then explaining it plainly. These days I'm doing two things I never had time for before: exploring my own town like a tourist who never leaves, and finally feeding the creative side I kept on a shelf for thirty years.

The Insider grew out of a simple itch. The more I poked around — the makers at the Sunday market, the cobbler who's resoled boots since 1990, the family that ran the same lunch counter for a century — the more I realized the best of Chattanooga is hiding in plain sight, and nobody was telling those stories the way the people behind them deserve. So I started doing it myself.

Two promises:

1. We work for you, not advertisers. The Insider takes no ads and no sponsorships — ever. When I tell you a place is worth your Saturday, it's because I believe it. Full stop.

2. We champion the people building this city. The independent shops, the makers, the founders, the folks behind the counter — the ones who make Chattanooga, in my admittedly biased opinion, the best town in America.

If that's your kind of thing, subscribe — the free edition lands every Wednesday. And if you know a person or place I ought to meet, reply to any issue; I read every email. I'm not chasing an exit here. I'm chasing the connections — the deeper ones, with the people quietly making this a remarkable place to live.

See you out there.
