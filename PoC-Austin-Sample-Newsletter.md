## 🦴 THE LEAD — Thinking about getting a pet? Austin makes it easy.

If you've been on the fence, you live in one of the best cities in the country to adopt. **Austin Animal Center is the largest no-kill municipal animal shelter in the United States**, sheltering **more than 18,000 animals a year**, and it has maintained no-kill status since 2011 — the city mandates a live-release rate of at least 95%. Walk-in adoptions are available **daily, 11 a.m.–7 p.m.** (adoption lines close at 6 p.m.). *(Source: City of Austin)*

Two more trusted, no-kill options:
- **Austin Pets Alive! (APA!)** — a foster-driven rescue where you can usually request a meet-and-greet and, once matched, often take your new family member home the same day. Ask about **Second Chance Saturdays**. *(Source: APA!)*
- **Austin Humane Society** — Austin's longest-standing no-kill, nonprofit adoption center, with Sponsor-a-Pet and Adopt-a-Kennel ways to help even if you can't adopt today. *(Source: Austin Humane Society)*

**New-adopter tip:** all three can help match a pet's energy level to your lifestyle — be honest about your space, schedule, and activity level, and lean on their staff and fosters. They do this every day.

## ❤️ WHY IT MATTERS — The science of the human-animal bond

It's not just a feeling. Research compiled by the **Human Animal Bond Research Institute (HABRI)** finds that **98% of pet owners report at least one positive health benefit** from having a pet, and documented effects of human-animal interaction include **lower blood pressure, reduced anxiety, and shifts in well-being-linked hormones like cortisol and oxytocin.** HABRI also estimates pet ownership saves the U.S. health-care system roughly **$22.7 billion a year**, and a global survey found **95% of people worldwide consider pets part of the family.** *(Source: HABRI)*

**The takeaway for Austin:** the bond is real, measurable, and a genuine public-health asset — one more reason our city's pet-friendly culture is worth protecting.

## 🏡 PET-INCLUSIVE HOUSING — Good news (and the fine print) for Austin renters

Finding a home that welcomes your pet is a real challenge nationwide: **72% of renters report difficulty finding pet-friendly housing and 59% say it's unaffordable**, and while ~79% of rentals "accept pets," **fewer than 10% allow them with no breed or size restrictions.** The encouraging part for us: **Texas is among the most pet-friendly states in the country, with roughly 91% of rental properties accepting pets** — among the highest rates nationally. *(Source: Michelson Found Animals, Pet-Inclusive Housing Initiative)*

**For renters:** ask specifically about **breed and weight limits and pet fees** (not just "do you allow pets?"), and get the policy in writing.
**For housing providers:** pet-inclusive policies pay off — residents in pet-inclusive housing **stay up to 21% longer**, reducing turnover and vacancy costs. *(Source: Michelson Found Animals)*

## ⏳ LIVE LONG & PROSPER — Adding healthy years to your pet's life

A few evidence-based ways to help your pet live longer and better:

- **Mind the mouth.** By age three, **70–80% of dogs and cats already show signs of dental disease**, which is linked to heart and kidney problems when untreated. Regular dental care genuinely adds healthy years. *(Source: AAHA)*
- **Watch the waistline.** An estimated **1 in 2 pets is overweight or obese**, raising the risk of diabetes, arthritis, and a shorter life. *(Source: AAHA)*
- **Don't skip the senior check-ups.** Veterinary guidelines define "senior" as the **last ~25% of a pet's expected lifespan**; twice-yearly visits catch problems early, when they're most treatable. *(Source: AAHA)*
- **Size matters for dogs.** The **Dog Aging Project** — a major long-term study of companion-dog aging supported by the National Institute on Aging — notes that **smaller breeds generally live markedly longer** (a Chihuahua can reach 15–20 years; giant breeds like Great Danes average ~6–8). The project is even running a clinical trial (TRIAD) testing whether a compound called rapamycin can extend healthy lifespan in large dogs. *(Sources: Dog Aging Project; NIH/NIA)*

## 🎾 OUT & ABOUT — Where to play this week

Austin is built for dogs. A few local favorites:
- **Red Bud Isle** — a ~13-acre **off-leash island** on Lady Bird Lake, surrounded by water on three sides; a longtime local favorite for swimming and romping.
- **Auditorium Shores** — a **4.5-acre off-leash area** right on Lady Bird Lake, steps from the paved **Ann & Roy Butler Hike-and-Bike Trail** that loops the lake (~10 miles, on-leash).
- **Yard Bar** (N. Lamar) — an off-leash dog park paired with a bar and kitchen, and a regular host of pet-benefit events.
*(Sources: Austin Parks Foundation; Visit Austin; BringFido)*

> ⚠️ **Safety first:** Austin's lakes — including Lady Bird Lake (Red Bud Isle, Auditorium Shores) — can develop **toxic blue-green algae** in warm months, which has sickened and killed dogs in past years. **Always check the City of Austin's current algae advisory before letting your dog swim.** *(Source: City of Austin)*
