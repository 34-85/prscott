◆ Lead Signal · Triangulation

The roll-up era is ending — capital is fleeing multi-brand debt and flowing to focused concepts

Read the bankruptcy dockets next to the M&A and it's one story: the securitized, many-brand roll-up is breaking, while private equity pays up for single, fast-growing concepts.

COLLAPSING FAT Brands — Chapter 11 (Case 4:26-bk-90126, S.D. Tex.), ~$1.3B debt, 18 brands / 2,200+ units; now filing a ~$446M liquidation plan. HIGH
Shari's (Lena Brands) — Chapter 11 (D. Del.), down to 9 units after ~86 closures. MED
On the Border — owner moves from selective to full chain closure after its 2025 Ch. 11 "rescue." MED
CONSOLIDATING Roark Capital bought a majority of Dave's Hot Chicken at ~$1B (targeting up to 4,000 units). HIGH
Denny's taken private by a TriArtisan-led group, ~$620M EV, closed Jan 16, 2026. MED-HIGH

THE READ  The 2010s playbook — stack many brands on cheap securitized debt — is unwinding in the bankruptcy filings. The money isn't leaving hospitality; it's rotating to focused, single-concept growth stories (hot chicken, a beloved diner taken private to fix it offstage). Watch which orphaned FAT/Shari's brands get bought, and by whom.
